What is happening

You did not lose yourself because you became a mother.

You lost yourself because no one told you it would ask this much. The pressure did not just increase. It multiplied.

Sleep deprivation. Constant touch. Noise. Invisible labor. Responsibility without pause. Somewhere in the middle of managing everyone else, you stopped being able to manage yourself the way you used to.

Suddenly you are more reactive, snapping faster, feeling touched-out, cycling through guilt, overstimulated by the end of the day, and wondering why everyone else seems to be handling what you cannot.

This is not a character flaw. It is a nervous system under strain.

Feeling disconnected from your body.
Struggling with anxious or intrusive thoughts.
Building resentment in your partnership.
Missing who you were before motherhood.

There is a name for what you are going through. Matrescence.

Matrescence is the psychological, hormonal, neurological, and relational process of becoming a mother. It is a developmental transition as significant as adolescence, reshaping your identity, your body, your stress response, and your capacity.

The therapy space

You look fine. You are functioning. But underneath, there may be a slow erosion.

By the time the house goes quiet, you may feel like someone you do not recognize, with nothing left for yourself.

That is not laziness. That is depletion. The work of therapy is not about trying harder. It is about understanding what has been running you and finally having somewhere to set it down.

The good news? You are here. And you are not alone.

Our work together

Support that meets the weight of what you are carrying.

The Regulated Mother is not mom advice. It is cognitive, somatic, and identity-integrated therapy for women navigating matrescence.

This work is structured, grounded, and emotionally deep. It is not surface-level coping strategies.

With support, you stop white-knuckling through the hard moments. You snap less, and when you do, you know how to come back. You recognize what you need before it turns into resentment. You find your voice in your relationship again.

This is not about becoming a perfect mother. It is about becoming one who can come back to herself.

The SELF Method

The SELF Method in motherhood.

Shed the Story

Uncover the beliefs driving your reactivity: good moms do not get overwhelmed, I should be able to handle this, everyone else manages, why cannot I?

Embrace the Fire

Stop running from the hard emotions. Make space for rage, grief, anxiety, resentment, and overstimulation without shame.

Liberate Your Voice

Reclaim your needs and set boundaries in your relationship, with your kids, and with yourself.

Forge a New Way

Build capacity instead of chasing perfection. Repair instead of collapse. Show up as the mother and person you want to be.
