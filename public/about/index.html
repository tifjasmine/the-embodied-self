About Tiffany

The person behind The Embodied Self.

I am Tiffany McGilloway, a Licensed Psychotherapist who blends traditional evidence based talk therapy with body-based, mindfulness-informed healing. My work is for people who are tired of carrying everything alone and are ready to build a more honest, compassionate relationship with themselves.

A blend of clinical training, creativity, and embodied practice.

My approach

Compassionate, direct, and rooted in the whole self.

I do not believe therapy has to feel cold or overly clinical. I believe therapy can be a space where you feel safe enough to be honest, supported enough to go deeper, and challenged enough to grow.

We look beneath the symptom.

Anxiety, burnout, self-criticism, people-pleasing, resentment, and disconnection often make more sense when we understand the story underneath them.

We include the body.

The body often holds what the mind has learned to explain away. We may use grounding, breath, awareness, movement, or nervous system support when it feels useful.

We rebuild self-trust.

Together, we work toward a relationship with yourself that feels less harsh, less performative, and more honest, compassionate, and rooted.

Get to know me

The human part belongs here too.

Therapy is professional, but I believe it should also feel deeply human. You are not sitting across from a blank wall - you are working with a real person who brings warmth, creativity, groundedness, curiosity, reflection, and a little playfulness into the room. My hope is that this helps therapy feel honest and supportive, while still leaving space for depth, movement, self-discovery, and the kind of healing that helps you come home to yourself.

Life outside therapy

How I spend my free time

Being a mommy to two beautiful little humans, growing as a woman, partner, therapist, and business owner, creating whenever I can, and learning constantly. Honestly, learning might be one of my favorite hobbies.

What I wish more women knew

You do not have to earn rest.

You do not have to prove your worthiness or carry everything alone. Your enoughness is not something you achieve. You are allowed to choose yourself without apology, rest without guilt, and love yourself without permission.

Places that shaped me

Countries I have traveled to

Indonesia, Thailand, Jamaica, Spain, Italy, France, the United Kingdom, Mexico, China, Malaysia, the Bahamas - and counting. Thailand was especially meaningful because I spent five months there deepening my relationship with healing, movement, and myself.

Travel lessons

What traveling has taught me

Happiness is not hidden in faraway places. It is something you carry with you. You can find joy with very little, peace in unexpected moments, and clarity when you finally slow down. The world has taught me that life is choreography - a dance of people, places, and quiet pauses that shape who we become.

Another lifetime, same calling

If therapy were not my career

I would still find some way to teach, heal, and perform. I started teaching movement in high school - gymnastics, acrobatics, yoga, dance - and I never really stopped. That will always be part of me. I love the joy of telling a story without words.

A lesson life keeps teaching me

Peace is something I choose.

Life keeps reminding me that I always have the option to respond differently, breathe deeper, and come back to myself before reaching for anything outside of me.

Healing favorite

My most-used app for healing

Insight Timer. It is my favorite meditation app, hands down - and it is free. I love having simple practices available for grounding, reflection, breath, sleep, and returning to myself.
